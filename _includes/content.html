About me

I am a fifth year Ph.D student at UMass Amherst, in the Department of Mathematics & Statistics. I am co-advised by Professor Markos Katsoulakis and Professor Luc Rey-Bellet.

My Research

I am currently studying different ways to quantify the errors associated with splitting schemes in parallel Kinetic Monte Carlo.

In general, the point of contact of the different projects I am working on is to employ tools from information theory in new ways to generate insights and quantify uncertainties.

Data Science

Due to my interest in data science, I co-founded the Graduate Researchers interested in Data (GRiD) group. I served as Co-Chair of the group during the fall semester of 2014 and the spring semester of 2015. As a Co-Chair, I helped organize UMass Amherst HackEbola and the 2015 ASA DataFest. I am currently the group's Chief Technical Officer.

I am also an assistant organizer for the Western Mass Data Science, Stats, and R group.
