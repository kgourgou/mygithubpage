Hello

My name is Kostis Gourgoulias and, as of June 2017, I'm working with the Data Science / AI team at babylon health in London, UK. Before that, I was conducting research that led to a PhD in (Applied) Mathematics at UMass Amherst, advised by Professors Markos Katsoulakis and Luc Rey-Bellet. Research Interests

My research interests lie in applied mathematics, probabilistic modeling, and statistics. I am particularly interested in problems that are interdisciplinary and combine computations and mathematical thought. Recently, I have also been thinking a lot between the interplay between data science, machine learning and numerical analysis.

My thesis can be condensed to the following points:

  • Trying to pick an appropriate numerical scheme for your problem? This can be interpreted as model selection!
  • Information criteria (IC) can be designed that can capture computational/theoretical trade-offs, e.g., communication between different processors in parallel computing, computer memory budget, fidelity of model, etc.
  • IC can also capture model bias over a family of quantities of interest.
